Personal Story

The Journey

My path into chip verification started with a fascination for digital systems and the precision required to prove that complex hardware actually behaves as intended. That curiosity grew into a career built around one of the most demanding corners of VLSI engineering — functional verification of high-speed protocols and processor cores.

Early in my career I focused on building a solid foundation in UVM-based environments — learning how constrained-random stimulus, functional coverage, and layered testbench architecture come together to catch real bugs in real designs. What drew me deeper into the field was the debugging side: understanding why silicon fails, tracing anomalies through LTSSM state machines, reset sequences, and SerDes layers to find the root cause.

A defining chapter was my involvement in PCIe Gen5 subsystem verification. Getting a high-speed serial link from link training through stable enumeration and transaction-level operation is one of the most technically demanding verification tasks in the industry. Working through LTSSM edge cases, SerDes sensitivity, and power management scenarios gave me a depth of protocol understanding that carries into every subsequent project.

Alongside PCIe, I have worked extensively on AXI MEM-SS, DMA controllers, NoC fabrics, and peripheral IPs — building and enhancing UVM testbenches, closing coverage with IMC, and identifying corner-case RTL bugs that constrained-random alone would not reliably find. I have also developed assembly-driven verification flows for RISC-V cores using golden reference models, an approach that demands precise understanding of both the ISA and the implementation.

Two-plus years in, I remain motivated by the same thing that drew me to this work: the confidence that comes from thorough verification — knowing that the IP you hand off has been genuinely exercised and is ready to go into silicon. Joining Squared Studio is an opportunity to bring that rigour to a wider range of challenging projects, working alongside engineers who share the same standards.
