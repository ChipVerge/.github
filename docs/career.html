Trainee RTL Design Engineer

RTL Design Full-time Remote Entry Level
Apply Now
Role Overview

This is an entry-level position for engineers looking to build a serious career in ASIC RTL design. You will work directly alongside senior engineers on real production-grade RTL for ASIC and FPGA targets — writing synthesizable SystemVerilog, participating in code and lint reviews, and gradually taking ownership of design blocks. Expect hands-on learning, structured mentorship, and zero tolerance for sloppy code.

Responsibilities
  • Write clean, synthesizable RTL in SystemVerilog under senior guidance
  • Implement and integrate digital design blocks per micro-architecture specifications
  • Run and interpret lint, CDC, and simulation results; fix violations independently
  • Participate in internal design reviews and respond to review feedback
  • Maintain structured, comment-annotated code ready for synthesis handoff
  • Document micro-architecture decisions and module interfaces
Requirements
  • B.Sc. in Electrical Engineering, Electronics, or Computer Engineering (or equivalent)
  • Solid understanding of digital design fundamentals: combinational and sequential logic, FSMs, timing
  • Working knowledge of Verilog or SystemVerilog — coursework or personal projects accepted
  • Ability to read and understand existing RTL code and a micro-architecture document
  • Familiarity with simulation concepts — understand what a waveform tells you
  • Strong written communication in English for technical discussion and documentation
Good to Have
  • Any FPGA board experience — Basys, Nexys, Arty, DE10, or similar
  • Exposure to a logic simulator (ModelSim, QuestaSim, Verilator, Icarus)
  • Basic awareness of synthesis flows (Vivado, Quartus, or DC)
  • Awareness of CDC, reset-domain crossing, and metastability concepts
  • Any RTL project on GitHub — even a simple CPU or UART implementation counts
  • Familiarity with Python or Tcl for scripting
Questions? Write to info@chipverge.com

RTL Design Verification Engineer

Verification Full-time Remote 1–3 Years Experience
Apply Now
Role Overview

We are looking for a verification engineer who can build and operate UVM testbenches on real block-level and subsystem-level designs. You will own verification environments end-to-end: architecture, stimulus generation, scoreboarding, coverage closure, and regression. You should be comfortable debugging deep protocol failures, writing meaningful coverage groups, and explaining sign-off status to the design team. This role runs in parallel with active ASIC engagements — quality and schedule discipline are non-negotiable.

Responsibilities
  • Develop and maintain UVM testbenches for RTL blocks and subsystems
  • Write constrained-random sequences and directed test cases targeting spec-defined corner cases
  • Develop scoreboards, monitors, and functional coverage models
  • Achieve and document functional and code coverage closure against agreed targets
  • Run and triage automated regressions; isolate and root-cause failures to the RTL or TB
  • Write and maintain SVA property checks for protocol compliance and design intent
  • Produce verification plans, test reports, and sign-off checklists
Requirements
  • B.Sc. in Electrical Engineering, Electronics, or Computer Engineering (or equivalent)
  • 1–3 years of hands-on verification experience in an industrial or serious academic setting
  • Proficiency in SystemVerilog for testbench coding — interfaces, clocking blocks, classes
  • Working knowledge of UVM: agents, sequences, scoreboards, factory, phasing
  • Experience with functional and code coverage: covergroups, cross coverage, exclusion management
  • Ability to read RTL, understand design intent, and derive independent test scenarios
  • Strong debugging skills — proficient with waveform tools and simulation logs
Good to Have
  • Experience with formal property verification (JasperGold, VC Formal, or similar)
  • Familiarity with standard interface protocols: AXI, AHB, APB, PCIe, or similar
  • Python-based verification experience (cocotb, pytest-based regression)
  • Awareness of CDC verification flows and structural analysis tools
  • Knowledge of DFT-aware verification and scan-mode test integration
  • Any published or open-source UVM environment, even a personal project
Questions? Write to info@chipverge.com
